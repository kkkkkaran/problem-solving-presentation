Step 1 - Visualize the End Goal

Start with the destination in mind

  • What does success actually look like?
  • What problem are we really solving?
  • Who benefits and how?

For our dispatch system:

  • Efficient warehouse operations
  • Real-time tracking of jobs
  • Automated carrier integration
"If you don't know where you're going, any road will get you there."

Real Example - Working Backwards

Dispatch System Dependencies

Critical Path:

  • Job creation/validation
  • Warehouse & device assignment
  • Shipping label generation
  • Carrier integration

Dependencies:

  • Shipping Carrier Interface
  • Inventory Service
  • Printing Service
  • Webhook notifications

Key insight: We needed a strategy pattern for shipping carriers

Created ShippingCarrierManager to abstract AusPost vs StarTrack implementations

"By starting with the end-to-end dispatch process and working backwards, we identified the key integration points we needed to solve first."

Step 3 - Break It Down

Create achievable components

  • Divide by function
  • Create logical groupings
  • Keep components manageable (1-2 day tasks)
"The best way to eat an elephant is one bite at a time."
This is basically how I approach eating a burrito bowl too

Once we had the pieces, it's still a puzzle - but one you can actually solve

Putting It All Together

1
Clear vision: End-to-end warehouse dispatch system
2
Work backwards: Identify critical services & interfaces
3
Break it down: SOLID services with clear responsibilities

Architecture highlights:

  • Interface-based ShippingCarrier with concrete implementations
  • Dedicated PrintingService and InventoryService
  • Observer pattern with DispatchJobObserver for webhook events
  • Strategy pattern in ShippingCarrierManager
Yes, I know this looks suspiciously like "draw the rest of the owl" but I promise it works

Why This Works

Team Benefits:

  • Clearer communication
  • More accurate estimates
  • Easier delegation

Technical Benefits:

  • Faster identification of roadblocks
  • Higher quality solutions
  • More maintainable code

For our dispatch system, specific benefits included:

  • Service classes with single responsibilities
  • Polymorphic shipping carrier implementations
  • Clear interfaces between components
  • Easier testing with well-defined boundaries
"Our dispatch system achieved clean separation of concerns with services that follow SOLID principles."

Start Small

Try this approach on your next PHP challenge:

1
Take 15 minutes to write out what success looks like
2
Identify your 3 most critical integrations/dependencies
3
Break the problem into logical controllers, models and services

In our dispatch system, this approach gave us:

  • Specialized controllers for each aspect (Jobs, Devices, BoxSizes, etc.)
  • Clean model relationships with clear responsibilities
  • Modular services that could be independently tested
If you try this approach and it doesn't work, you still have my permission to blame your product manager
